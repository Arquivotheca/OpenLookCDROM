XView Programs

Here is a list of the main XView-based programs in this section. We've compiled most of them for both Linux and SunOS4. The SunOS4 binaries are dynamically linked, since this is the standard format for programs. Since SunOS 5.x is upwards compatible(:-)), you can run most or all of these programs on Solaris 2/SunOS 5. However to do so you must have installed the "Binary Compatibility Option" (BCP).

A problem with Linux

But on Linux there is no single standard format for executables. Or rather, too many standards. Linux is undergoing a transition in the format of its executable programs, from its own ad-hoc executable format to the System V Release 4 standard "ELF" format. As a result, we are predicting a certain amount of confusion over libraries and have, therefore, chosen with some reluctance to link the demo copies of these programs statically. This means they will work on almost any Linux in the world, whether from Linuxes like Yggdrasil (which used -libX11.so.3.* in their Fall 1994 release) or certain others (which used -libX11.so.6), or even systems which have converted to ELF (the kernel will long support the old executable format).

But they may run slowly, especially from CD-ROM. On Linux, your best bet is to copy the programs to your hard disk. You may also wish to rebuild them, either dynamically (for you, we left the Linux .o files in the source tree! And there is an updated XView 3.2 library in lib.linux in the CD-ROM) or recompile everything for ELF (to do either of these, you may need to copy the src/xview tree or parts of it to your hard disk, or use a tree of symbolic links).
